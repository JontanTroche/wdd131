WDD 131 - Dynamic Web Fundamentals

1. Site Name

ShredLog: Guitar Mastery Tracker

Description: I chose this name because it combines the term "Shred" (associated with technical and fast guitar playing, common in genres like progressive metal) with "Log" (record), which clearly defines the application's purpose.

Why I chose it: As a guitarist with 15 years of experience, I am looking for a tool that projects professionalism and discipline. The name is short, easy to remember, and communicates that the site is for musicians who are serious about their technical progress.

Optional Domain Availability: shredlog.app / guitarmastery.io

2. Site Purpose

The purpose of ShredLog is to provide a specialized digital environment for guitarists to organize, track, and analyze their practice routines. The site offers:

  • A centralized dashboard to monitor weekly goals and overall progress.
  • A real-time practice timer with categorization (Scales, Repertoire, Theory, Improvisation).
  • A historical data log to visualize technical growth and maintain long-term discipline.

3. Target Audience

The target audience for this website includes:

  • Intermediate to Advanced Guitarists: Specifically those focused on high-technical proficiency (shredding, prog-metal) who need to measure their speed and accuracy gains.
  • Music Students: Individuals enrolled in formal music programs who need a reliable way to log their mandatory practice hours.
  • Self-Taught Musicians: Hobbyists seeking structure and visual motivation to avoid "plateaus" in their learning journey.

Audience Needs: These users require a fast, mobile-responsive interface that doesn't distract them from their instrument but provides meaningful data about their efforts.

4. Scenarios

The following questions represent the needs of my target audience and guide the content of the website:

  • "How can I track my daily practice time to ensure I am meeting my weekly goal of 10 hours?"
  • "Can I categorize my practice sessions to see if I'm spending too much time on theory and not enough on technical exercises like scales?"
  • "Is there a way to save a quick note about a specific difficulty I faced during my session so I can focus on it tomorrow?"

6. Typography

Following the Google Fonts Choosing Guide, I have selected two font families that balance aesthetic "personality" with technical functionality:

Font 1: Montserrat (Headings & Navigation)

Application: H1, H2, H3, and Main Navigation buttons.

Justification: As a Geometric Sans-Serif, Montserrat projects a modern, tech-focused personality that aligns with the "Shredding" and "Gear" culture of guitarists. Its high x-height and bold weights make it an ideal Display font for short, high-impact text like page titles and time displays.

Font 2: Roboto (Body Text & Practice Logs)

Application: Paragraphs, list items, table data (logs), and form labels.

Justification: Roboto is a "workhorse" font known for its neutral yet friendly appearance. Because the app will handle a "flood of words" in the notes section and complex numerical data in the practice logs, Roboto's high legibility at small point sizes (16pt and below) ensures the user remains focused on their data without eye strain.
